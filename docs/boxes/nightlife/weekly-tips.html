Weekly Tips

TIPS FOR THE WEEKEND 27-28th JUNE IN FLORENCE




EVENTS AND EXHIBITIONS:


Open-air cinema: there are loads of outdoor movie screenings and venues where you can enjoy a film under the stars. Some great options include Apriti Cinema, with its huge screen in front of Palazzo Pitti; the Cinema at the Cloister of Sant'Orsola; Villa Bardini, with its stunning views over Florence; Manifattura Tabacchi; the garden of Conventino Caffè Letterario; and plenty more. Check out the full list here: https://firenzeurbanlifestyle.com/cinema-all-aperto-firenze-2026-arene-estive/

Museo Diffuso: Want to see Florence from above and explore hidden views of the city? Discover Florence's history and how the city grew over time by visiting its towers, gates, and fortresses — including Porta San Frediano, Torre della Zecca, Torre San Niccolò, and Fortezza da Basso. More info at www.musefirenze.it.

Exhibitions you shouldn't miss: at Palazzo Strozzi, the major exhibition dedicated to Rothko, plus in the courtyard you'll find the installation There are other fish in the sea by Superflex, reflecting on climate change; the Museo Novecento presents Baselitz. AVANTI!, a major exhibition dedicated to Georg Baselitz, one of the leading figures in contemporary art; Cattedrale dell'Immagine presents the immersive exhibition "Inferno," dedicated to Dante Alighieri; at Palazzo Medici Riccardi, the exhibition Firenze Decò. Atmospheres of the 1920s will be on display; and finally, at Villa Bardini, you can also visit Florence '50s '60s '70s, a photographic journey through three decades of Florentine history featuring images from the Foto Locchi Archive.

The Arno River: if you want to experience Florence from the water, here are a few fun options: take a ride on the traditional Renaioli boat and glide under Ponte Vecchio (www.renaioli.it); try paddleboarding (www.toscanasup.org); or, if you're feeling adventurous, go rafting (www.firenzerafting.it).

Estate Fiesolana: Set in the stunning 1st-century BC Roman Theatre of Fiesole, this summer festival brings together music, theatre, dance, and cultural talks in a truly unique atmosphere. For more information, visit estatefiesolana.it.



MARKETS AND EVENTS:


Antiques Market: head to Piazza Annigoni to browse stalls packed with antiques, collectibles, books, paintings and all kinds of vintage treasures. You'll also find retro design pieces, curiosities and unique finds.

Santo Spirito Market: on Sunday, Piazza Santo Spirito fills with stalls selling handmade crafts, antiques, books, clothing, vintage items, local delicacies and regional food specialties.

Comics & More: over the weekend, Piazza dei Ciompi hosts a market dedicated to comics, vinyl records, CDs and vintage goodies — perfect for anyone who loves retro finds, collecting and Florence's pop culture scene.

Japanese Festival: this weekend, Villa Vogel welcomes back Tanabata, the Japanese Star Festival. Expect a market with Japanese crafts and accessories, fortune-telling, calligraphy workshops, delicious food, matcha green tea and plenty of live performances.

Ars Manualis – The Legacy of Craftsmanship: Piazzale Michelangelo hosts an artistic crafts market celebrating traditional skills and showcasing the very best of handmade excellence.

Florentine Summer: tasty street food, live music, aperitivo, events and much more! Summer in Florence means enjoying the city's many outdoor venues, from Molo on Lungarno Colombo and Fico Bistrot in Le Cure, to Love Orticoltura in the beautiful Giardino dell'Orticoltura, Anconella Garden, Habana 500 for a taste of Cuba by the Arno, Fuoribordo in Florence South, Light in Campo di Marte, Limonaia di Villa Strozzi, The Lodge below Piazzale Michelangelo, Atollo on Lungarno Aldo Moro and Lumen on Via del Guarlone.
