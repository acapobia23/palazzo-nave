Weekly Tips

TIPS FOR THE WEEKEND 23-24th MAY IN FLORENCE




EVENTS AND MUSEUMS:


European Night at the Museums: This Saturday, museums all across Europe will take part in the event, with special evening openings and tickets for just €1 (where admission is normally paid) at major museums, monuments, parks, and state archaeological sites. In Florence, participating venues include the Accademia Gallery, the Uffizi, the Opera del Duomo Museum, the Bargello Museum, San Marco, and many more.

National Day of Historic Houses: On Sunday, hundreds of historic residences — together forming Italy's largest "open-air museum" — will open their doors to visitors. It's a great chance to explore beautiful and historic places that are often off the usual tourist trail. For more info: Associazione Dimore Storiche Italiane.

The Arno River: If you want to experience Florence from the water, here are a few fun options: take a ride on the traditional Renaioli boat and glide under Ponte Vecchio (www.renaioli.it); try paddleboarding (www.toscanasup.org); or, if you're feeling adventurous, go rafting (www.firenzerafting.it).

Exhibitions you shouldn't miss: At Palazzo Strozzi, the major exhibition dedicated to Rothko, plus in the courtyard you'll find the installation There are other fish in the sea by Superflex, reflecting on climate change. The Museo Novecento presents Baselitz. AVANTI!, a major exhibition dedicated to Georg Baselitz, one of the leading figures in contemporary art. At Palazzo Medici Riccardi, the exhibition Firenze Decò. Atmospheres of the 1920s will be on display. And finally, at Villa Bardini, you can also visit Florence '50s '60s '70s, a photographic journey through three decades of Florentine history featuring images from the Foto Locchi Archive.



MARKETS:


Santo Spirito Market: On Sunday, Piazza Santo Spirito will be filled with stalls selling handmade crafts, antiques, books, clothing, and vintage finds, along with local goodies like honey, cheese, and much more (H 9/19).

Comics & Vintage Vibes: Head to Piazza dei Ciompi for a retro-style weekend market packed with comics, vinyl records, CDs, and vintage clothing (H 9/20).

Fashion in Flair: Over the weekend, Le Serre Torrigiani will host a stylish Italian craft market featuring 40 artisans from all over Italy. Expect unique handmade pieces displayed in the historic Italian garden and the beautiful 19th-century greenhouse — all celebrating creativity, originality, and true Made in Italy style (H 10/19:30).

Smile Market: This weekend at Manifattura Tabacchi you'll find big brands, special deals, and a curated selection of clothing, accessories, bags, shoes, jewelry, watches, and more (H 9/21).

Piazza in Arte: On Saturday, the charming artisan market returns to historic Piazza Ognissanti, along the right bank of the Arno, with local crafts and artistic creations on display.
