Weekly Tips

TIPS FOR THE WEEKEND 13-14th JUNE IN FLORENCE




EVENTS AND EXHIBITIONS:


Calcio Storico Fiorentino: the Greens of San Giovanni vs. the Reds of Santa Maria Novella and the Whites of Santo Spirito vs. the Blues of Santa Croce will face off in the 2026 edition of Florence's famous historical football tournament this weekend. This intense and spectacular sport dates back to the 15th century and takes place on a sand-covered pitch set up in Piazza Santa Croce. After a colorful historical parade featuring Renaissance costumes, the four neighborhoods of Florence battle it out in a unique mix of football, rugby, and combat.

Exhibitions you shouldn't miss: At Palazzo Strozzi, the major exhibition dedicated to Rothko, plus in the courtyard you'll find the installation There are other fish in the sea by Superflex, reflecting on climate change. The Museo Novecento presents Baselitz. AVANTI!, a major exhibition dedicated to Georg Baselitz, one of the leading figures in contemporary art. Cattedrale dell'Immagine presents the immersive exhibition "Inferno," dedicated to Dante Alighieri. At Palazzo Medici Riccardi, the exhibition Firenze Decò. Atmospheres of the 1920s will be on display. And finally, at Villa Bardini, you can also visit Florence '50s '60s '70s, a photographic journey through three decades of Florentine history featuring images from the Foto Locchi Archive.

The Arno River: If you want to experience Florence from the water, here are a few fun options: take a ride on the traditional Renaioli boat and glide under Ponte Vecchio (www.renaioli.it); try paddleboarding (www.toscanasup.org); or, if you're feeling adventurous, go rafting (www.firenzerafting.it).

Museo Diffuso: Want to see Florence from above and explore hidden views of the city? Discover Florence's history and how the city grew over time by visiting its towers, gates, and fortresses — including Porta San Frediano, Torre della Zecca, Torre San Niccolò, and Fortezza da Basso. More info at www.musefirenze.it.

Estate Fiesolana: Set in the stunning 1st-century BC Roman Theatre of Fiesole, this summer festival brings together music, theatre, dance, and cultural talks in a truly unique atmosphere. For more information, visit estatefiesolana.it.



MARKETS AND EVENTS:


Creative Factory: This weekend, Piazza dei Ciompi comes alive with a curated selection of artisans, designers and makers, celebrating creativity and a passion for handmade craftsmanship. (H 9:30/20)

Ognissanti in festa: In the square of the same name on Saturday, the market exhibition dedicated to vintage collecting and creative craftsmanship awaits you — unique and unmissable opportunities, but also a journey through memories and creativity. Hand Made. (H 9/20)

Fierucolina del Carmine: Sunday morning market in Piazza del Carmine, perfect for local food shopping with products from small producers including bread, pasta, vegetables, fresh olive oil, wine, fruit, honey, flour, and cheese. (H 8/13)

Arts & Crafts in Oltrarno: On Sunday, the square fills up with lots of stalls selling handmade crafts, antiques, books, clothing, and vintage items, as well as local specialties like honey, truffles, and much more. (H 9/19)

Florentine Summer Vibes (Estate Fiorentina): Summer in Florence means open-air venues, tasty food, live music, aperitivo time and plenty of activities. Check out some of the city's favorite summer spots: Molo on Lungarno Colombo, Fico Bistrot in Le Cure, Love Orticultura in the beautiful Orticoltura Garden, Anconella Garden on Via di Villamagna, Habana 500 for a Cuban atmosphere by the Arno, Fuoribordo in the south of Florence, Light in Campo di Marte, Limonaia di Villa Strozzi, The Lodge below Piazzale Michelangelo, Atollo on Lungarno Aldo Moro and Lumen on Via del Guarlone.
