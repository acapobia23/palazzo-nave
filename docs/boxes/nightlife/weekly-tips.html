Weekly Tips

TIPS FOR THE WEEKEND 25-26th APRIL IN FLORENCE




EVENTS, MUSEUMS, AND GARDENS:


April 25th is celebrated as Liberation Day, marking Italy's freedom from Nazi occupation and Fascism, and honoring the Italian Resistance against Nazism and Fascism. There will be lots of events, demonstrations, and parades, and Florence will be filled with red to remember the partisan struggle.

Special openings and free museums: State museums and archaeological parks will be open for free. The Uffizi Gallery, the Accademia, and Boboli Gardens will have special free openings, and the Medici Chapels will also have free entry.

Gardens to visit in spring: The Iris Garden is reopening, where around 3,000 varieties of hybrid irises from all over the world are in bloom. At Villa Bardini you can admire Florence's main landmarks surrounded by the scent and beauty of wisteria in bloom, an incredible display of lilac, purple, and pink stretching 70 meters long and 4.5 meters wide. Then there's the vast Medici park of the Boboli Gardens for an open-air museum experience among grottos, fountains, panoramic views, and the Camellia Garden; the scenic Rose Garden with over 1,000 plant varieties, including 350 ancient roses; and the charming 19th-century English-style romantic park of the Stibbert Museum Garden, with grottos, terracotta statues, and Egyptian and Hellenistic temples; and finally, the Medici Park of Pratolino, full of grottos, statues, fountains, and water features.

Exhibitions not to miss: At Palazzo Strozzi there's a major exhibition dedicated to Rothko, and in the courtyard you'll find the installation There are other fish in the sea by Superflex, reflecting on climate change. The Museo del Novecento presents Baselitz. AVANTI!, a major exhibition on Georg Baselitz, one of the leading figures in contemporary art. The Tornabuoni Gallery hosts a monographic exhibition on Mario Ceroli, Myth and Matter. The Museo degli Innocenti features a large exhibition on Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec, a master of the Belle Epoque and a symbol of late 19th-century Paris. At the Cattedrale dell'Immagine you can enjoy a 35-minute immersive experience exploring the artistic and personal world of Frida Kahlo. Palazzo Medici Riccardi presents Firenze Deco. Atmospheres of the 1920s. Villa Bardini and the Stefano Bardini Museum host an exhibition dedicated to the American dancer Isadora Duncan.



MARKETS AND EVENTS:


MIDA, International Crafts Fair: From 25th April to 3rd May the International Crafts Fair will be held at the Fortress, where tradition and innovation, creativity and technology come together and transform into exclusively handmade masterpieces by some of the best artisans in the world (H 10/22).

Market Exhibition of plants and flowers: From 25th April to 3rd May at the Horticulture Garden there will be a wide variety of plants and flowers from more than 75 exhibitors from all over Italy (H 9/19:30).

Vintage market: The usual market in Piazza dei Ciompi in Florence, dedicated to comics, vinyl, clothing, vintage, and collectibles, returns with dozens and dozens of stalls from many parts of Italy (H 9/20).

Santo Spirito in Fiera: The market of organic products and artisanal creations, with antiques, books, clothing, and much more, returns to Piazza di Santo Spirito on Sunday 27th April (H 9/19).

Ars Manualis - the heritage of craftsmanship: Arrives in Piazza Strozzi with an artistic crafts market, bringing top-quality handmade excellence right into the square (H 10/20).

Collecting in Florence: Over the weekend, at the parking area of Teatro Cartiere Carrara (formerly Tuscany Hall), the exhibition-market dedicated to collectibles, small antiques, and modern antiques returns (H 10/19).

Fierucola dei Legnaioli: On Sunday, Piazza Ognissanti is all about wood lovers and artisans - carpenters, carvers, turners, chair makers, sculptors, artists, toy makers, and researchers - where you can buy unique handmade items that reflect the history and character of the trees they come from (H 9/19).
