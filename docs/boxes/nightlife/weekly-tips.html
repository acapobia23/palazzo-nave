Weekly Tips

TIPS FOR THE WEEKEND 30-31th MAY IN FLORENCE




EVENTS AND EXHIBITIONS:


Exhibitions you shouldn't miss: At Palazzo Strozzi, the major exhibition dedicated to Rothko, plus in the courtyard you'll find the installation There are other fish in the sea by Superflex, reflecting on climate change. The Museo Novecento presents Baselitz. AVANTI!, a major exhibition dedicated to Georg Baselitz, one of the leading figures in contemporary art. Cattedrale dell'Immagine presents the immersive exhibition "Inferno," dedicated to Dante Alighieri. At Palazzo Medici Riccardi, the exhibition Firenze Decò. Atmospheres of the 1920s will be on display. And finally, at Villa Bardini, you can also visit Florence '50s '60s '70s, a photographic journey through three decades of Florentine history featuring images from the Foto Locchi Archive.

The Arno River: If you want to experience Florence from the water, here are a few fun options: take a ride on the traditional Renaioli boat and glide under Ponte Vecchio (www.renaioli.it); try paddleboarding (www.toscanasup.org); or, if you're feeling adventurous, go rafting (www.firenzerafting.it).

Gardens to visit in spring: The panoramic Rose Garden oasis, home to more than 1,000 plant varieties including 350 ancient roses; the charming 19th-century English-style romantic park of the Stibbert Museum Garden, with caves, terracotta statues, and Egyptian and Hellenistic temples; and finally the Medici Park of Pratolino, filled with grottos, statues, fountains, and water features.



MARKETS AND EVENTS:


Cantine Aperte: A whole weekend to enjoy with great wine, art, music, and fun — from vineyard tours with an expert in Etruscan culture, to picnics among the vines featuring the wineries' latest wines, plus countryside walks to discover the beautiful views that make Tuscan vineyards so unique.

Mercatino dell'Antiquariato: Piazza Annigoni fills with stalls selling vintage and collectible items, books, paintings, and all kinds of antiques; among the stands you'll find modern antiques, design pieces, and plenty of vintage curiosities.

Only Usato Market: On Saturday at Giardino dell'Orticultura and Sunday at Il Molo by the Arno, the event dedicated to second-hand and vintage shopping is back, with market stalls, open artisan workshops, and lots of music (H 12/22).

Artigianato d'Autore: The beautiful Chiostro della Calza hosts this market exhibition dedicated to high-end craftsmanship, where you'll find tailored clothing, jewelry and accessories, bags, illustrations, home décor, furniture pieces, and tableware (H 11/20).

Fiera del Disco: Head to Manifattura Tabacchi for a true musical deep dive and browse a huge selection of vinyl records (33s and 45s), mixes, and CDs covering all kinds of music genres (H 10/20:30).
