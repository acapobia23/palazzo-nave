Weekly Tips

TIPS FOR THE WEEKEND 16-17th MAY IN FLORENCE




EVENTS, GARDENS, AND EXHIBITIONS:


The Arno: To discover Florence from a different perspective and literally walk on the river waters, we recommend: a ride on the historic Renaioli boat (www.renaioli.it), paddling under Ponte Vecchio on a SUP board (www.toscanasup.org), and for the more adventurous, a rafting excursion (www.firenzerafting.it).

Gardens to visit in spring: The Iris Garden reopens, where around 3,000 varieties of hybrid irises from all over the world bloom; the panoramic Rose Garden oasis, home to more than 1,000 plant varieties including 350 ancient roses; the charming 19th-century English-style romantic park of the Stibbert Museum Garden, with caves, terracotta statues, and Egyptian and Hellenistic temples; and finally the Medici Park of Pratolino, filled with grottos, statues, fountains, and water features.

Exhibitions you shouldn't miss: At Palazzo Strozzi, the major exhibition dedicated to Rothko, plus in the courtyard you'll find the installation There are other fish in the sea by Superflex, reflecting on climate change. The Museo Novecento presents Baselitz. AVANTI!, a major exhibition dedicated to Georg Baselitz, one of the leading figures in contemporary art. At Palazzo Medici Riccardi, the exhibition Firenze Decò. Atmospheres of the 1920s will be on display. And finally, at Villa Bardini, you can also visit Florence '50s '60s '70s, a photographic journey through three decades of Florentine history featuring images from the Foto Locchi Archive.



MARKETS AND EVENTS:


Indipendenza Antiquaria: This weekend, Piazza Indipendenza will host 110 stalls featuring furniture, books, prints, sculptures, collectibles, paintings, and much more (H 9/19).

Ars Manualis: "The Heritage of Craftsmanship" arrives in Piazza Santa Maria Novella with an artistic crafts market celebrating excellence in traditional handmade skills (H 10/20).

East Market Firenze: Manifattura Tabacchi will host this event for the first time, featuring more than 100 exhibitors selling vintage items, design pieces, vinyl records, and more, alongside installations, music, and street food (H 10/21-23).

Ludicomix 2026: In Empoli, the weekend will feature the festival dedicated to gaming, video games, comics, and illustration, with exhibitions, stands, food, and special events.

Photo Happening: Villa Vogel in Florence will be the heart of the event, hosting four photography exhibitions set up in the Limonaia and the Cloister.

Wine Weekend: The world of wine comes to Scandicci, where Parco dell'Acciaiolo will become a true capital of taste, with over 70 wineries from across Italy and more than 400 wines available for tasting (H 11/19:30).

Fierucolina di Santo Spirito: On Sunday, Piazza Santo Spirito will host more than 60 exhibitors, including small producers and artisans selling local food products and handmade crafts (H 9/19).

Greve in Fiore: Piazza Matteotti in Greve in Chianti will be filled with stalls dedicated to plants, flowers, and everything related to gardening (H 9/19:30).

Sapori e Colori: On Sunday, the center of Fiesole — where visitors can enjoy panoramic views over Florence — will host a market featuring handicrafts and collectibles (H 8/20).

Only Usato Market: On Sunday, Fico Bistrò will once again host the market dedicated to second-hand and vintage goods, with stalls, artisan workshops, and plenty of music (H 12/22).
